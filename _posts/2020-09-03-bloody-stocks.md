# 9/3/2020 - A Bloody Day For Stocks
Last week's 5-for-1 stock split by Tesla spurred a manic run-up in the market that brought its stock price to a pre-split equivalent of $2,300. There was a kind of manic energy around it with a lot of good news on the horizon: S&P 500 listing, Battery Day, and various production process improvements. 

It seemed like we were headed to the moon. Getting off at this point would mean getting left behind by the rocket.

The rally turned out to be short-lived and as of Thursday night, it is trading at $380 or a $1900 pre-split equivalent.

I remember when it was in the $900 range and I thought that it would be hard to see it go any higher. Now that I've seen it go to $2,300 and down to $1,900, I am thinking that it really has the potential to get to $5,000.

I've seen these kinds of moves before and I think the stock will go up again, especially with the good announcements yet to come. It's fun to speculate about selling near the latest high and then rebuying as the price goes lower. Perhaps after Battery Day?
